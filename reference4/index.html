The hazards present in the chemical and petrochemical industries, in the most part, are hidden from view. We have not developed the instincts for protection, and the hazards need to be studied and positive steps have to be taken to prevent disasters. A typical petrochemical plant such as this there are very many potential sources of hazard, many obvious, some not so obvious. We have all seen the results where, usually through a combination of circumstances it all goes horribly wrong. We can take measures to protect from electrical hazards but in some situations no amount of protection will help.

The ignition triangle is a simple illustration of the basic elements of an explosion. If any one of these elements can be reliably eliminated, then an explosion is prevented. In this context we might speak of a gas it includes vapours as well. Dust can include, Dust, fibres or flyings which can be suspended in the air or as a layer. There are three groups, Group I is for use in mines susceptible to firedamp. Group II is for places with a explosive gas atmosphere other than mines susceptible to firedamp. Group III is for places with a explosive dust atmosphere other than mines susceptible to firedamp. Expo only produces equipment suitable for Group II and Group III. Equipment marked IIB is suitable for applications requiring group IIA and IIB equipment. Equipment marked IIC is suitable for applications requiring group IIA, IIB and IIC equipment.

Combustible flyings are solid particles, including fibres, greater than 500 micrometers in nominal size which may be suspended in air and could settle out of the atmosphere under their own weight. Examples are cotton, jute, cocoa fibre. Both conductive and non conductive dust are finely divided solid particles, 500 micrometers or less in nominal size, which may be suspended in air or settle out of the atmosphere under its own weight. They may burn or glow in air or form explosive mixtures with air at ambient pressure and normal temperatures. The difference between the two types of dust is there electrical resistivity. Non conductive is greater than 103Ωm, conductive is equal or less than 103Ωm. Equipment marked IIIB is suitable for applications requiring group IIIA and IIIB equipment. Equipment marked IIIC is suitable for applications requiring group IIIA, IIIB and IIIC equipment.
